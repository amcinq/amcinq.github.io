2020 UGV Full Redesign

Sept. 2020 - Present (ongoing)

At the beginning of the Fall 2020 semester, I began working with an interdisciplinary team within CUAir to quickly redesign our Unmanned Ground Vehicle with the goal of efficiently increasing our scoring capability with an improved rover design.
The Unmanned Ground Vehicle (UGV) is dropped from the aircraft at altitude and guided to the ground in an unpowered descent. The UGV must land within a 5 ft radius for full points, carrying a water bottle as a payload.
The 2020 redesign was grounded in the lessons of the past, which included the following lessons and corresponding design decisions:
  • The parafoil, used to control the UGV's descent, worked inconsistently and was difficult to control. → We decided to use a new method for descending (see below).
  • The UGV was heavy and had trouble surviving the drop. → Produce a lighter UGV with stronger materials, such as carbon fiber and/or sheet metal.
  • The UGV project has a long and complex history, and most design efforts moved slowly. → Create something testable within a month of the project start.
With our goals set, we moved forward with the first stage in our process - deciding the descent method. Given that the parafoil had reached a dead end, it was time to try something fresh. We had two main options: drop the UGV with a parachute with no control, or drop the UGV with a controlled glider wing. In order to evaluate which option was the best, a mechanical team member and I conducted a trade analysis. The analysis sought to answer two main questions:
  1. Is a glider wing feasible from a wing area standpoint?
  2. How accurately must the plane drop a UGV with a parachute in order to score points?
To test the feasibility of the glider wing, I used MATLAB to model the wing area needed for straight-and-level glider flight as a function of UGV weight and airspeed. The graph on the left shows that the required wing area is reasonable; for our purposes, we estimated that a wing area of 300-400 m2 was a reasonable prediction.
